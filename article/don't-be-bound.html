Leonardo Da Vinci: Do not be bound by anything

He Who possesses most must be most afraid of loss.

If you have much, your fear of losing it will be greater. This advice serves as a reminder that we should not base our lives on possessions. If your life depends on possessions, it will be filled with the fear of loss.

We often connect ourselves to things outside of ourselves using words like "have" or "own." The same applies to wealth, "I now have a MacBook, this MacBook is mine." Eventually, there will come a time when you face the loss of your MacBook, whether it is stolen or broken. When your MacBook breaks, you will feel extraordinary sadness because you had previously declared that the MacBook was yours. Religion often advises us, reminding us not to depend on anyone but God, as dependency on anything else will lead to fear and eventually disappointment. Everything besides God will inevitably leave us, regardless of what it is. Therefore, if you want to live peacefully, reduce attachment to anything. Wealth, status, family, and so on, it doesn't mean you can't be close to them. Who says you can't be close? The key is not to be attached. Being attached means being dependent, usually tested with phrases like "if not." "If not for you, my life would be in darkness," "If not for this MacBook, I can't work," "If not for smoking, my mind feels dizzy, smoking makes me feel calm." These are signs of dependency. Once there are words like "if not," it means you are dependent, there is possession there and you have the potential to feel disappointed, to feel loss, and to feel fear, all of it combined.

Leonardo da Vinci said, He who possesses most must be most afraid of loss. Therefore, those most anxious about losing wealth are the rich, the rich are more afraid of losing their money. Meanwhile, those who have little have less fear, what would they fear losing if they have nothing? Hence, do not be easily dependent.

He who thinks little errs much: All this time, we have believed that thinking too much leads to many mistakes. This is not true. According to Leonardo, it is quite the opposite; if you rarely think, you will often make mistakes. Why is that? Because you are not practiced in thinking. So, it is fine if you think incorrectly; it can be corrected later. If you make a mistake, it can be fixed. Keep thinking, don't worry; eventually, you will become skilled in critical thinking and thinking correctly. If you rarely think, your mind is seldom used, meaning you are still prone to err in your thinking. Therefore, do not be afraid to think, because those who think little actually have a greater potential for making mistakes.

He who truly knows has no occasion to shout: Anyone who truly knows the truth does not feel the need to shout. Those who are still noisy about their truth usually do not fully believe in it themselves. People who are preoccupied with pointing out others' mistakes often doubt themselves and seek to assert their own correctness. If someone still needs to assert their own correctness, it actually means they are not entirely confident in their own truth.

Typically, when you know the truth, you are calm and not argumentative. "You can say whatever you want, I already know what the absolute truth is." However, if you are still argumentative, still eager to identify others' mistakes, it usually means you still need justification for your own truth, which lies in others' errors.

Therefore, we must be cautious with the truth we hold, as sometimes we become preoccupied with finding others' faults. This is a sign that we are not fully confident in our own truth. If we are truly confident in our truth, we no longer need to prove that others are more wrong than we are, that others are mistaken and we are the only ones who are right. This is one of Leonardo's principles: those who are still noisy in seeking out who is wrong are likely not entirely confident in their own truth.

In the tradition of Islamic philosophy (Ilm al-kalam), there is a term "sukunun nafs" (tranquility of the soul). One of the parameters of truth is sukunun nafs. When you have reached the terminal of truth, your soul is at peace because you know what is absolute.
